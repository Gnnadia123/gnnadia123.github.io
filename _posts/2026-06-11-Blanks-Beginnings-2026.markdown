On Saturday, 6 June 2026, I took part in Blank's Beginnings, a competition held annually by Anglo-Chinese School's VEX V5 robotics club, 8059Blank. The part-scrimmage, part-tournament event saw 25 teams taking part, including schools like Nan Hua High School (8501A), UWCSEA Dover (13965), myself with SST (8068), and ACSI themselves (8059). Competing under the plate 8068S Isomagnetic, we stormed through qualifications, initially having a 4-1-0 WLT. However, unfortunate incidents in the last 2 matches ended up pulling our W-L-T ratio down to 4-3-0, Ultimately dropping us from 3rd seed to 9th seed. 

We were picked by 8059S to take the 5th seeded alliance, while our sister teams 8068V Isochromatic allianced with 8501A Bullet to take 1st seed, and 8068E Isodynamic allianced with 8059E to take 8th seed. In the end, our BB26 journey ended early as we ultimately lost in quarterfinals to 4th seeded alliance 81819C and 81819E. 8068V Isochromatic was also eliminated in the quarterfinals, while 8068E stormed through to emerge as Tournament Champions against SCRT and 8059X. 

Having just landed in Singapore in the wee hours of the morning, the sleep deprivation was apparent. However, I still pushed through, performing consistently well in every match. I learnt how important it is to be resilient, and to stand strong even when everything you can think of is to give up. I'm definitely glad I chose to sacrifice soome sleep and fight the fatigue for 8 hours to attend this competition.
