This page showcases 8068Y Isomagnetic's IDE early-season Override robot. 

Our IDE robot has many new improvements as compared to the previous generation from [Blank's Beginnings BB26](https://gnnadia123.github.io/2026/06/11/Blanks-Beginnings-2026.html). Some improvements include the toggle, claw, lift, drivetrain etc, which will be talked about below. 

# Drivetrain

During Blank’s, our robot was running on a 44W drivetrain with a mix of omni-wheels and traction wheels. For IDE, we decided to switch to and experiment with a full omni-wheel drivetrain. This change was triggered after rewatching our matches, where maneuverability of our robot was crucial for toggle play, as the mobility gives us more freedom to move around the field. We also increased our power to 55W, to make full use of our drivetrain’s potential and power. 

# Lift

For IDE, we decided to switch our lifting mechanism from a double reverse 4-bar (DR4B) to a string cascade. This change was made after seeing how the center of gravity was lower and more centralised, leading to less risk of tipping in matches. Furthermore, a cascade requires less materials to build, allowing us to complete our robot faster and with less resources used. Cascade lifts are also more rigid and stable as compared to a DR4B, ultimately giving us better build quality for our robot. 

# Toggle

Learning from past experience, we switched from a short toggle to a longer and more extensive toggle, as well as having the toggle on both sides of the bot, instead of only one like we did at Blanks. The extra toggle provides more symmetry for our robot, allowing us to toggle from a steeper angle of attack from either side, as well as make it more consistent. The toggle is supported by a half-cut brace which forms a triangle with the structure, increasing structural durability and resilience during a match.
